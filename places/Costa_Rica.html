Costa Rica

Costa Rica is a biologically megadiverse country. The varieties of species of all major clades is fascinating. Combined with the natural beauty (beaches etc.) and the culture makes this really a superb place.

Most people speak English in hotels and agencies so language barriers are not serious.

Manuel Antonio

This is quite a large park and you could actually spend up to 2-3 days here, but we did 2 days. We would recommend to get a guide since they will know about the animals and usually have a portable telescope with them to observe them. We arranged one beforehand, but you can also ask for one at the entrance.

Animals

Here you'll find 3 of the 4 monkey species of CR. They are all very fascinating. Capuchin monkeys are quite friendly and you do not need to be scared of them. Howling monkeys are more timid. You are also very likely to find sloths (2 spp.). Personally I really liked the coatis. They are fox-like mammals that are very indifferent to human presence and walk around the park. We saw an entire family with babies. There are also lots of birds, insects and reptiles the guide can show you.

White-headed capuchin (Cebus capucinus).

Common house gecko (Hemidactylus frenatus). These are small reptiles that often appear on walls or rocks.

Sloth sanctuary

This is a place for sloths that need veterinary assistance (sloths are protected by CR law). It was very fun. You can see the sloths up close and the tour is interesting.

Brown-throated sloth (Bradypus variegatus) (a species of three-toed sloth) under the care of the sanctuary.

Corcovado

Corcovado is a large tropical rainforest on the Pacific coast. If you have enough time, it is definitely with coming here. However, it is geographically difficult to access. We got there by boat starting from Sierpe and then we went to Drake Bay. We stayed at the Sirena, which is a station in the forest. They have basic amenities there but it is expensive to buy food there, so would recommend taking a few things. Meals are organised in the canteen.

Animals

Here you can find all 4 species of monkeys. There are also some aquatic reptiles. We luckily saw a tapir plus an anteater.

Drake Bay - places to eat

Before taking the boat ride we ate at Claudio's Grill. They had excellent food.

Geoffroy's spider monkey (Ateles geoffroyi). These monkeys have long limbs and use their tails to grab branches.

Baird's tapir (Tapirus bairdii).

Ecuadorian hermit crab (Coenobita compress). These are everywhere on the beach and inside the forest. Our guide told us these was even one inside a bottle cap.

Cahuita

Cahuita is a national park on the atlantic coast. The animals can be seen as you go through a walking trail. You can also get a guide for the trail, or part of it.

Great kiskadee (Pitangus sulphuratus).
