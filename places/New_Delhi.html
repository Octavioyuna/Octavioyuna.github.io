New Delhi

New Delhi has a multitude of interesting sites. Be aware that the city center is quite chaotic and is not easily navigable due to dense human traffic. During rush hour, also be aware that the traffic becomes almost unpracticable.

National Museum

This is one of the main museums of archeology and history. It contains several famous artefacts, such as the 12th c. bronze Nataraja of Shiva, as a 'cosmic dancer'. You can get a guide in the entrance.

Humayun's Tomb

This is the tomb of the Mughal Emperor Humayun, built 1569-70. It is a great example of Mughal Indo-Islamic architecture. The history of the tomb and the Mughal Emperors is very interesting.

Qutb Minar

Qutb Minar is the world's second tallest minaret and stands on the site of the Quwwat-ul-Islam mosque, which was the first mosque built in Delhi after the Islamic conquest of India. Some of the columns have Hindu-style architecture.
