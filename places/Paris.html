Paris

Paris is one of the major cities in Europe and is one of the most visited cities in the world. It is extremely rich in culture and impressive architecture. Most of the mainstream tourism is localised to certain hotspots which are justifiably busy. However they are worth visiting in spite of the crowds. Furthermore, you can still get a very nice feel of the city by going to less touristy places.

The underground is the fastest and easiest way to get around.

Notre Dame Cathedral

The cathedral is conveniently quite central and easily accessible.

Notre Dame.

Tour Eiffel

Be prepared for large crowds here, depending on the season. You can get to the higher level either by elevator or on foot.

Tour Eiffel close up.

Jardins de Luxembourg

These are some very beautiful large gardens with scenic fountains, pools, classical statues and buildings. It is definitely worth a visit.

Jardins de Luxembourg.

The Louvre

The Louvre is an enormous museum. Several days can be spent here. There is a collection of classical art in wings according to time and geographical origin. It's the home of the famous Mona Lisa, arguably the most famous painting in human history. You may see a crowd next to it and it is smaller than you might expect.

Sacré-Cœur Basilica

The Sacré-Cœur Basilica is located at the summit Montmartre, a large hill in Paris's northern 18th arrondissement.

Sacré-Cœur Basilica.

Centre Pompidou

This is an exhibition hall which contains a collection of modern and contemporary art as well as an interesting architecture.

Palace of Versailles

The Palace of Versailles (French: Château de Versailles) was a former royal residence commissioned by King Louis XIV. It is one of the largest palace complexes in Europe. You can get here easily via a regional train.

Cafes

Paris is famous for its cafes and pastry. There are many scattered throughout the city. A "café gourmand" combines the traditional coffee at the end of a meal with at least three different mini desserts.
