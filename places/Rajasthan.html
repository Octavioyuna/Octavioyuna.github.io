Rajasthan

Rajasthan is a federal state of India bordering Pakistan. It has a unique history and culture due to heritage from the Mughal Empire and other cultures. There is a multitude of palaces, forts and temples.

The Hindu temples are among the most beautiful attractions here. In general, I would recommend to carry around some little plastic shoe covers to visit the Hindu temples, as sometimes shoes are prohibited but socks or bare feet with plastic covers are allowed.

City Palace, Udaipur

The large palace complex on the bank of Lake Pichola was first built in the 1500s by the Maharanas (Kings). It is possible to obtain a guide at the entrance who will explain the history of the palace and the artwork.

Pandit Deendayal Upadhyay Park right next to the palace is very beautiful and has resident monkeys. The grey monkeys here are relatively pacific, as opposed to the more red-faced macaque monkeys. There is also the possibility of a lake ride to Jag Mandir, a small palace on the lake.

Mount Abu and Dilwara Temples

The Dilwara temples contain some of the most impression stonework I have ever seen. The temples are very meticulously carved with geometric patterns and jain deities. Photography is not permitted inside. There is a hiking path that goes to a hill offering a viewpoint of the mountains and a peculiar rock formation called the toad rock.

You can get there from Udaipur with a taxi.

Other Temples of Udaipur

Jagdish Temple, Udaipur

This temple is easily accessible from the city center.

Eklingji Temple

This is a popular temple complex where the Hindu deity Shiva is worshipped. In the central area of the temple, a large icon of Shiva in black can be seen. Photography isn't permitted.

Sahastra Bahu Temples

I found these temples particularly beautiful due to the surroundings.

Ahar Cenotaphs

These are a series of cenotaphs and chhatris near the city center. What I enjoyed about them is that you are free to visit and walk around without very large crowds.

Jaipur

Jaipur is Rajasthan's capital city. There are a lot of temples and forts around the city.

Jantar Mantar

This is a very interesting museum full of ancient astronomical and scientific equipment. It contains the world's largest sundial.

City Palace

This is one of the main palaces. It contains a well-known entrance arch that is adorned with elephants.

Amer Fort

The Hindu-style architecture here is very unique.

Jaigarh Fort

This is a fort that overlooks the Amer fort. It is famous for its set of ancient cannons.

Nahargarh_Fort

This fort has 2 monkey species that reside around it. Red-faced macaque monkeys are aggressive and one needs to be careful.

Albert Hall Museum

We only saw this museum at night from outside. The building is really impressive and ornate.

Hawa_Mahal

This is a palace in the city center. You can visit inside with guides that you can find near the entrance.

Jal Mahal

This is a palace in the middle of the lake.

Birla Mandir, Jaipur

This Hindu Temple is particulary impressive because of the distinctive white stone it was built with.
