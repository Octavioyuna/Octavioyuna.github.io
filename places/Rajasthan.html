Rajasthan

Rajasthan is a federal state of India bordering Pakistan. It has a unique history and culture due to heritage from the Mughal Empire and other cultures.

The Hindu temples are among the most beautiful attractions here, although In general, I would recommend to carry around some little plastic shoe covers to visit the Hindu temples, as sometimes shoes are prohibited but socks or bare feet with plastic covers are allowed.

City Palace, Udaipur

The large palace complex on the bank of Lake Pichola was first built in the 1500s by the Maharanas (Kings). It is possible to obtain a guide at the entrance who will explain the history of the palace and the artwork.

Pandit Deendayal Upadhyay Park right next to the palace is very beautiful and has resident monkeys. The grey monkeys here are relatively pacific, as opposed to the more ref-faced maaque moneys There is also the possibility of a lake ride to Jag Mandir, a small palace on the lake.

Mount Abu and Dilwara Temples

The Dilwara temples contain some of the most impression stonework that I have ever seen. The temples are very meticulously carved with geometric patterns and jain deities. There is a hiking path that goes to a hill offering a viewpoint of the mountains and a funny rock formation called the toad rock.
